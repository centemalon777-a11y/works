Detailed Chapter Summary

The chapter opens dramatically at the morning assembly of Stardom Schools. The principal, Mr. Bepo, walks unusually slowly toward the podium — immediately catching the attention of students and teachers.

When he takes the microphone, he cannot speak. Instead, he bursts into tears before the entire school. Students are shocked because Mr. Bepo is known as a strong, respected, and cheerful leader.

The Vice Principal, Mrs. Grace Apeh, tries to find out what is wrong, but Mr. Bepo remains silent. She then asks Mr. Justus Anabel, the Chemistry teacher, to end the assembly and send students back to class.

After the students leave, staff escort Mr. Bepo to his office. Despite numerous attempts by teachers and the school nurse to comfort him, he continues crying for more than thirty minutes without explaining why.

As news spreads, worried parents begin calling the school. The Vice Principal eventually contacts the Managing Director (MD), Mrs. Ibidun Gloss, who is attending an event outside school.

Interestingly, the day had started positively. The school had reduced boarding fees from ₦250,000 to ₦165,000, improving punctuality significantly. Before the breakdown, an SSS1 student Ikenna Egbu had delivered an inspiring speech about an excursion to Jos — receiving loud applause.

Mr. Bepo is respected for his 24 years of dedicated service. His humorous imitation of characters from the TV drama Village Headmaster earned him the nickname "The Lekki Headmaster."

Mrs. Ibidun Gloss returns quickly. She is especially surprised because the previous day had been a celebration — the school achieved a 90% WASSCE success rate and teachers received bonuses. She asks Mr. Ope Wande, a Physics teacher and pastor, to counsel Mr. Bepo — without success.

The chapter ends with the mystery unresolved, leaving readers eager to discover what caused Mr. Bepo's breakdown.

Key Points
1
Mr. Bepo's Emotional Breakdown
The principal unexpectedly cries during assembly and refuses to explain the reason — shocking the entire school community.
2
Mystery Introduced
Readers are left wondering what caused his distress. This becomes the chapter's central suspense and narrative hook.
3
School Environment
Stardom Schools is portrayed as disciplined and achievement-oriented. Academic excellence is highly valued by the management.
4
Leadership and Concern
Teachers and management show genuine concern for Mr. Bepo, demonstrating a culture of care within the institution.
5
Importance of Reputation
The MD worries about how the incident may affect the school's image and public standing in the community.
6
Reward and Accountability
Teachers are rewarded for success (90% WASSCE pass rate) and criticized for poor performance — showing balanced management.
7
The Lekki Headmaster's Reputation
Mr. Bepo is respected for 24 years of dedication, leadership, and humor — making his breakdown all the more alarming.
